## Interpretation

- The larger 96-pair cohort keeps the same overall story as the 18-pair cohort.
- The minimal direct route remains real and strong enough for a clean mechanistic claim.
- The fuller late writer bundle still gives slightly better explanatory power than the minimal route.
- `H28` looks like a genuinely useful late helper on the larger cohort.
- `H2` has a mixed role: it hurts the sufficiency-style patching result, but it helps the necessity-style ablation result.
- Current best writeup should present `L12H15/H5/H4/H28` as the cleaner sufficiency-oriented late carrier while noting that `H2` still contributes under ablation.

## Family-Level Read

Using the new per-pair artifacts, removing `H28` weakens path patching across every indicator family in the 96-pair cohort.
The largest drops were in:

- `Invoke-WebRequest`
- `-EncodedCommand`
- `DownloadFile`
- `DownloadString`
- `Invoke-Expression`

By contrast, removing `H2` is neutral or slightly helpful in most families.

The ablation-side story is different. Under grouped ablation, `H2` helps most for:

- `DownloadFile`
- `DownloadString`
- `Invoke-WebRequest`
- `-EncodedCommand`

It is close to neutral or slightly anti-causal for:

- `FromBase64String`
- `Invoke-Expression`
- `IEX`

So `H2` should currently be described as a family-sensitive auxiliary late head rather than a stable core writer.

## Evasion Benchmark: First Circuit Follow-Up

I ran the first focused circuit follow-up on the only technique that produced real misses in the conservative evasion benchmark:

- Technique: `downloadstring_psobject_invoke`
- Cohort: 6 `DownloadString` seed/variant pairs
- Actual misses: 2/6, both in the `FromBase64String` family

### Seed vs Variant Classification

- Seed mean logit diff: `2.0755`
- Variant mean logit diff: `0.9193`
- The two misses both landed at variant logit diff `-0.09375`

### Early-Head Ablation: `L0H11 + L0H9`

This does not look like an evasion mechanism that works by suppressing the early detector heads.

### Path Patching Follow-Up

I then ran sufficiency-style path patching on the same 6 pairs.

| Route | Cohort | Mean delta | Flip rate | Read |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|
| `L0H11 -> L12H15/H5/H4` | Original seeds | `-2.3385` | `66.67%` | Strong minimal direct route |
| `L0H11 -> L12H15/H5/H4` | Obfuscated variants | `-1.2552` | `66.67%` | Weaker, but still clearly present |
| `L12H15/H5/H4/H28` | Original seeds | `-2.6771` | `100.00%` | Stronger fuller late carrier |
| `L12H15/H5/H4/H28` | Obfuscated variants | `-1.6901` | `83.33%` | Also weaker, but still strongly sufficient |

Per-pair read on the 2 missed variants:

- `3537__seed_833f6cd11ac6__downloadstring_psobject_invoke.ps1`: minimal-route patch delta `+0.0625`, late-carrier patch delta `-0.40625`
- `3537__seed_59fd6d3c0181__downloadstring_psobject_invoke.ps1`: minimal-route patch delta `-0.71875`, late-carrier patch delta `-1.53125`

So the current best interpretation is more precise:

- `downloadstring_psobject_invoke` does not mainly evade by turning off `L0H11/L0H9`
- It also does not erase the direct route or late carrier entirely: both still show strong sufficiency under path patching
- What changes is necessity: the late `L12H15/H5/H4/H28` bundle is causal under ablation on the original seeds, but becomes weak or anti-causal under ablation on the obfuscated variants
- The cleanest current read is that this obfuscation reroutes computation around the usual late carrier rather than deleting the route altogether

## H28 Isolation On The Evasion Cohort

I followed up by comparing `L12H15/H5/H4` against `L12H15/H5/H4/H28` on the same 6 `DownloadString` seed/variant pairs.

### Grouped Ablation

| Bundle | Cohort | Mean delta | Read |
|---|---|---:|---|
| `L12H15/H5/H4` | Original seeds | `-0.4219` | Real but weaker necessity |
| `L12H15/H5/H4/H28` | Original seeds | `-0.7214` | `H28` increases necessity on the unobfuscated seeds |
| `L12H15/H5/H4` | Obfuscated variants | `+0.3594` | Strongly anti-causal overall |
| `L12H15/H5/H4/H28` | Obfuscated variants | `+0.2240` | Still anti-causal overall, but less so with `H28` included |

For the two missed `FromBase64String` variants:

- top-3 ablation delta: `+0.8594`
- top-4 (`+H28`) ablation delta: `+0.3594`

So `H28` is not what makes the late bundle fail under ablation. If anything, adding `H28` partially rescues the late bundle on the missed variants.

### Path Patching

| Bundle | Cohort | Mean delta | Flip rate | Read |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|
| `L12H15/H5/H4` | Original seeds | `-2.2344` | `50.00%` | Strong but weaker than top-4 |
| `L12H15/H5/H4/H28` | Original seeds | `-2.6771` | `100.00%` | `H28` materially strengthens sufficiency |
| `L12H15/H5/H4` | Obfuscated variants | `-1.1797` | `66.67%` | Still strongly sufficient |
| `L12H15/H5/H4/H28` | Obfuscated variants | `-1.6901` | `83.33%` | `H28` still strengthens sufficiency after obfuscation |

For the two missed `FromBase64String` variants:

- `3537__seed_833f6cd11ac6__downloadstring_psobject_invoke.ps1`
  - top-3 patch delta: `+0.09375`
  - top-4 patch delta: `-0.40625`
- `3537__seed_59fd6d3c0181__downloadstring_psobject_invoke.ps1`
  - top-3 patch delta: `-0.9375`
  - top-4 patch delta: `-1.53125`

So the sharper interpretation is:

- `H28` still helps on this evasion cohort
- the obfuscation is not selectively bypassing `H28`
- instead, the obfuscation seems to reduce the necessity of the usual late writer bundle while leaving a still-usable sufficient route in place
- this is strongest on the missed `FromBase64String` variants, where the late bundle becomes anti-causal under ablation but remains partly recoverable under path patching
